About me

Building things,
understanding why they work.

I'm a 25-year-old aerospace engineering student at Politecnico di Milano, finishing my MSc thesis on Flettner rotors — spinning cylinders that generate lift using the Magnus effect, with real applications in wind-assisted ship propulsion.

My path through engineering has always leaned toward the hands-on: wind tunnel experiments, CFD simulations from scratch, flight testing with Pipistrel. I find the most interesting problems live at the intersection of data and physical intuition.

Outside the lab — water polo captain for years, 22 countries visited, Erasmus in Seville. I believe how you collaborate matters as much as what you know.
